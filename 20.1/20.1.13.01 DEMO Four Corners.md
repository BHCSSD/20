## 1. "Grid-Locked" Movement

In the past, we moved sprites by changing `x` or `y` directly:

* `y -= 5` (Move Up)
* `x += 5` (Move Right)

But if our sprite **rotates**, pressing "Forward" should move it diagonally in the direction it is facing. To do this, we need to split our **Speed** into two parts: a horizontal part (**vx**) and a vertical part (**vy**).

---

## 2. The Math

Imagine your sprite's path is a diagonal line. That line is the **Hypotenuse** of a right triangle. To find the other two sides (**x** and **y**), we use Trigonometry:

* **Cosine (cos):** Tells us the **Horizontal (x)** ratio.
* **Sine (sin):** Tells us the **Vertical (y)** ratio.

### The Formulas:

* **vx = cos(angle) * speed**
* **vy = sin(angle) * speed**

---

## 3. Implementation Step-by-Step

### A. Setup the Angle Mode

By default, computers use Radians. Boo...

```javascript
function setup() {
  createCanvas(400, 400);
  angleMode(DEGREES)
}

```

### B. The Logic

Inside our movement function, we calculate our "Steps" (**vx** and **vy**) based on the current `rotation` of the sprite.

```javascript
// Calculate how much to move on X and Y
let vx = cos(sprite.rotation) * sprite.speed;
let vy = sin(sprite.rotation) * sprite.speed;

// Apply those steps to our position
sprite.cx += vx;
sprite.cy += vy;

```
